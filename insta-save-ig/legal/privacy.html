Privacy Policy — Insta Save IG

Effective date: 1 May 2026 Last updated: 1 May 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Zenith Marketing (“we”, “us”, “our”) handles information when you use the Insta Save IG mobile application (the “App”) and related backend services.

We designed Insta Save IG to work without a traditional account. We do not ask you to create an account or provide your name, email address, phone number, contact list, Instagram login, or Instagram password for core app features.

However, the App does process some information needed to provide the service, manage free usage limits, support subscriptions, prevent abuse, and troubleshoot errors. This includes pseudonymous app identifiers, quota records, purchase entitlement information, links you submit, and minimal technical diagnostics.

4. Information we process

4.1 Pseudonymous app identity

To manage free quotas and subscription checks without requiring a login, the App and backend may use pseudonymous identifiers. These may include:

  • an app-scoped principal ID issued by our backend;
  • a signed or opaque principal token;
  • hashed or app-scoped technical signals from the device or platform; and
  • local identifiers stored by the App to recognise the same installation.

These identifiers are not intended to reveal your real-world identity. They may still be considered personal data under some privacy laws because they can distinguish one app installation from another.

We do not intentionally use raw hardware serial numbers or advertising IDs for this purpose.

4.2 Subscription and purchase information

Purchases are handled by Apple App Store, Google Play, and RevenueCat. We do not directly collect or store your full payment card details.

We may receive or access purchase-related information needed to unlock paid features, such as:

  • whether your Pro entitlement is active;
  • subscription product identifiers;
  • store receipt or transaction status as handled by Apple, Google, and RevenueCat;
  • RevenueCat anonymous customer identifiers or entitlement identifiers; and
  • restore-purchase status.

Apple, Google, and RevenueCat may process purchase information under their own privacy policies.

4.3 Links and media-resolution inputs

When you paste a supported link into the App, the App may send the link or structured information derived from it to our backend so the App can fetch a preview or help save media.

We use submitted links only to provide the action you requested, troubleshoot failures, and protect the service from abuse. We do not intend to build a browsing profile, social profile, or contact graph from submitted links.

Private, protected, deleted, unsupported, or restricted content may not work.

4.4 Saved media and device permissions

The App may request access to photos, gallery, storage, or similar device permissions so it can save media to your device.

Media saved through the App is stored locally on your device unless you choose to back it up, share it, or sync it through another service outside our control.

We do not ask for your Instagram login credentials and you should not send them to us.

4.5 Operational diagnostics and backend logs

We may process limited technical logs and operational events, such as:

  • request timestamps;
  • app version and platform;
  • endpoint success or failure;
  • generic error codes;
  • quota decisions;
  • request IDs; and
  • security or abuse signals.

These diagnostics are used to keep the App working, investigate bugs, prevent abuse, and protect backend services.

Infrastructure providers may also temporarily process technical data such as IP addresses as part of ordinary server operation, security, and logging.

  • to prevent abuse, spam, scraping, or attacks against our backend;
  • to troubleshoot crashes, failed requests, and reliability issues;
  • to comply with legal obligations; and

10. Retention

We aim to keep information only as long as needed for the purposes described in this policy.

Typical retention approach:

  • Quota and principal records: kept while needed to recognise installations, enforce fair usage, prevent abuse, and support subscription linkage.
  • Operational logs and diagnostics: kept for a limited troubleshooting and security period unless longer retention is needed for abuse, security, or legal reasons.
  • Submitted links: used to perform the requested feature and may appear in temporary operational logs. We do not intend to permanently store submitted links as a user history.

If you want current retention details for your installation, contact us.
